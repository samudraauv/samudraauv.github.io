About

SAMUDRA abbreviated as SYSTEM OF AUTONOMOUS MARINE UNDERWATER DETECTION RESEARCH & ACTIVITIES is an Autonomous Underwater Vehicle (AUV) developed by a team of undergraduate students from AIACTR (formerly Ambedkar Institute of Technology) to compete in the AUVSI. SAMUDRA is based on the principle low cost and high efficiency.

  • 2010-2011

    The Begining

    Students of AIACT& R are masterminds of this project. Ashish Kumar Jha and Kamlesh Kumar led to the foundation of the group whose motive was to develop cost effective AUV and to be the first non american team to win this AUVSI AUV competition.

  • May 2011

    First AUV Challenge

    The Ocean Society of India, and IEEE Madras Chapter, the National Institute of Technology (NIOT), under Ministry of Earth Sciences, announced a competition for students pursuing engineering degree to visualize and design an autonomous underwater vehicle. The proposed design by team SAMUDRA was ranked among the top 5 out of 150 teams.

  • April 2012

    SAVe 2012

    NIOT decided to continue the efforts and SAVe—2012 announcement was made. The scrutinizing committee verified the Preliminary Design documents among 57 registrations and shortlisted 17 teams. The team Samudra reach to the final along with other 7 teams to demonstrate the working AUV model after an oral presentation on Critical Design Report.

  • February 2014

    SAVe 2014

    SAVe 2014 announcement was made in NIOT’s website and through leading National Newspapers. After submission of Preliminary Design documents by 14 teams Out of 27 registeration only five teams are allowed to demonstrate their AUV

    • Delhi Technology University.
    • SRM University chennai.
    • Ambedkar Institute of Advanced communication Technologies & Research.
    • Indian Institute of Technology Madras.
    • Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur.

    Indian Institute of Technology Madras is the winner of the competition.

  • january 2015

    SAVe 2015

    This year five teams who prepared their AUV and participated in final phase of competition by presenting their working model are Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur, National Institute Of Technology Rourkela, SRM University chennai, Ambedkar Institute of Advanced communication Technologies & Research, Hindustan University chennai, Indian Institute of Technology Madras. SRM university Chennai is the winner of contest this time.
